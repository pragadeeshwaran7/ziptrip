Challenge 1 - Question 5: Three-Column Layouts

Demonstrating 4 different ways in CSS to place three boxes horizontally where the left/right boxes are fixed at 100px and the middle box fills the remaining space dynamically without overlap.

Method 1: CSS Flexbox (Modern Standard)

Utilizes display: flex on the container. The left and right boxes are given a fixed width and prevented from shrinking (flex-shrink: 0), while the middle box is given flex-grow: 1 (or flex: 1) to absorb all remaining space.

Middle (Flex Fill)
Right (100px)

Method 2: CSS Grid (Modern Layout Engine)

Utilizes display: grid on the container. We define three columns: grid-template-columns: 100px 1fr 100px;. This explicitly sets the outer columns to 100px and the center column to take 1 fractional unit of the remaining space.

Left (100px)
Middle (Grid 1fr)
Right (100px)

Method 3: Absolute Positioning & Margins (Classic Responsive)

The container is relative-positioned. The left and right boxes are absolutely positioned (left: 0 and right: 0 respectively) with width: 100px. The middle box is static but given margin-left: 100px and margin-right: 100px so it naturally fills the center space without overlapping the positioned elements.

Method 4: Floats & Width Calc (Legacy Compatibility)

Floats the left box to the left, the right box to the right. The middle box is floated left and its width is dynamically calculated using width: calc(100% - 200px);. A clearfix is applied to the container so that it doesn't collapse.
